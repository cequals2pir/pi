Space Base, Basic Odds

Which sector is most likely to score?

Buy Sector [6] ship paying one, or [7] paying three?

Spacebase is a dice game (overview: Board Game Geek, manufacturer: Alderac, play free: Board Game Arena) with an interesting scoring mechanic. Players roll two six-sided dice and choose to score either as a single sum or as two faces. For instance, a player rolling ⚂ and ⚃ chooses to score either on "Sector" [7] alone or on both Sector [3] and [4]. This article presents the calculation of probability for each sector, and a method to compare different ships in different sectors paying different rewards.

The probability of an event is the ratio of the number of all possible successful outcomes to the number of all possible outcomes. All 36 possible outcomes for two dice are shown in the table below, and all possible successful scores on sector [5] (arbitrary example) are bracketed.

In the table above, rolls summing to 5 which score on Sector [5] are {curly} bracketed. Four such sums occur. Faces with 5 pips which score on Sector [5] are [square] bracketed. Twelve such faces occur. Those successful four and twelve outcomes are summed to 16, and then divided by 36, the number of all possible outcomes. The percent probability of scoring Sector [5] is calculated as:

{4 sums} + (12 faces) ⁄ 36  =  16 ⁄ 36  = 44%

Sector [5] effectively scores on 44% of rolls. Each sector's probability is shown in the second to last "%" row in the table below.

Sectors are compared using the "%" probability row. Sector [6] is seen to be the most likely sector to score, answering the first question posed in the beginning of this article. Sector [6] is only slightly more likely than Sector [5], but is almost three times as likely as Sector [7]. The great impact of the ability to choose faces is seen by comparing Sector [2] and [12]; snake-eyes and box-cars are equally likely, but scoring Sector [2] is ten times more likely than Sector [12].

The Equal Payout ("EP") row is useful for comparing ships in different sectors paying different rewards. The values are normalized to unit Sector [6] by dividing Sector [6] "F+S" by each sector's "F+S". The values are the awards necessary for each sector to payout equally. A sector [6] ship with a single (coin, rocket, planet) award will payout equal to a sector [12] ship paying seventeen (after an infinite number of rolls).

The minimum value occurring at Sector [6] reflects its status as the most likely sector. The entire left hand half of the graph is nearly horizontal, showing sectors [1] thru [6] to be approximately equally likely. The game design reflects this two piece structure; different types of ships are available for Sector [1] thru [6] (ex., ships that use cubes to increase sum) than are available for Sector [7] thru [12] (ex., ships that activate adjacent sectors). A Sector [7] ship needs to pay out almost three awards (actual 2.8) to payout equal to a single award Sector [6] ship answering the second question posed in the beginning of this article. The higher number sectors curve upwards to a maximum of seventeen at sector [12]. Note the extreme curvature; Sector [12] requires double the award of Sector [11].

Careful consideration of the fundamental probability structure discussed above will favorably inform any engine building strategy style.
